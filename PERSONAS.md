# PERSONAS.md

Target buyer personas for Noesis / OutSystems enterprise outreach. Every AI-generated message, sequence, or research brief in this repo must name one of these personas before writing copy.

## How to use this file

- Pick one persona before drafting anything.
- Lead with the persona's pain, not Noesis capabilities.
- If the persona is ambiguous, stop and ask.
- Do not mix personas in a single message.

---

## 1. VP of IT / CIO

### Context
- Owns enterprise IT strategy and budget.
- Reports to CEO or COO.
- Evaluated on uptime, cost control, delivery speed, risk.

### Top Pains
- Backlog of modernization projects with limited budget.
- Pressure to show AI and automation wins without adding headcount.
- Dependency on aging systems that block business agility.
- Risk of vendor lock-in and failed transformation programs.

### What They Care About
- Delivery certainty.
- Total cost of ownership.
- Compliance, security, auditability.
- Proof from peer companies in their vertical.

### Outreach Hooks
- Industry-specific modernization outcomes.
- Delivery speed with certified teams.
- Risk reduction through proven partner delivery.

### CTA Style
- Peer benchmark, short exec briefing, or referenceable outcome — not a demo.

---

## 2. Head of Application Development / Director of Engineering

### Context
- Owns internal app delivery, dev teams, and platform choices.
- Reports to CIO or VP IT.
- Evaluated on throughput, quality, developer productivity.

### Top Pains
- Too many requests, not enough developers.
- Legacy apps consuming most of the team's time.
- Hiring senior engineers is slow and expensive.
- Pressure to adopt AI-assisted delivery without breaking standards.

### What They Care About
- Developer productivity and velocity.
- Maintainability and governance.
- Fit with existing architecture and CI/CD.
- Proof that low-code scales for serious enterprise apps.

### Outreach Hooks
- Throughput gains from OutSystems + certified delivery.
- Specific enterprise app examples (not marketing fluff).
- How Noesis teams extend existing dev capacity.

### CTA Style
- Technical walkthrough, architecture conversation, or delivery pattern review.

---

## 3. Enterprise Architect

### Context
- Owns architectural standards and platform decisions.
- Highly technical, skeptical of vendor claims.
- Evaluated on long-term fit, interoperability, and risk.

### Top Pains
- Avoiding platforms that create lock-in or tech debt.
- Integrating with legacy ERP, core banking, or EHR systems.
- Justifying low-code inside a code-first culture.
- Keeping pace with AI and agentic architecture trends.

### What They Care About
- Integration patterns and API surface.
- Governance, security, and lifecycle management.
- Reference architectures and performance at scale.
- Exit strategy and portability.

### Outreach Hooks
- Integration depth with enterprise systems they already run.
- Proof points from large-scale OutSystems deployments.
- Agentic AI capabilities (Agent Workbench, Mentor AI) framed architecturally.

### CTA Style
- Architecture review, peer-to-peer technical conversation, detailed reference.

---

## 4. Head of Digital Transformation / Chief Digital Officer

### Context
- Owns cross-functional modernization initiatives.
- Reports to CEO, COO, or Board.
- Evaluated on business outcomes, not IT metrics.

### Top Pains
- Slow delivery from IT blocks business cases.
- Hard to show measurable ROI on transformation programs.
- Stakeholder fatigue from failed or slow initiatives.
- Need visible AI and automation wins.

### What They Care About
- Business outcomes tied to revenue, cost, or customer experience.
- Speed from idea to production.
- Executive-level proof stories.
- Change management and adoption.

### Outreach Hooks
- Outcome-first case studies (e.g., Credibom 18% faster credit processing).
- Speed-to-value narratives.
- Agentic AI tied to real business processes.

### CTA Style
- Peer story, executive roundtable, outcomes-focused briefing.

---

## 5. Head of Testing / QA Lead

### Context
- Owns test strategy, automation, and release quality.
- Reports to Head of App Dev or CIO.
- Evaluated on release velocity and defect rates.

### Top Pains
- Manual regression testing blocking releases.
- Test automation coverage gaps in critical apps.
- Pressure to adopt AI testing without disrupting existing pipelines.
- Shortage of skilled automation engineers.

### What They Care About
- Automation coverage and release confidence.
- AI-assisted test generation and maintenance.
- Integration with existing CI/CD.

### Outreach Hooks
- TestingON (AI-powered test automation) value proposition.
- Coverage and release velocity gains.
- Reduction of manual regression effort.

### CTA Style
- Technical demo, POC conversation, benchmarking.

## Vertical Overlays

Adjust tone and proof points based on the buyer's vertical.

### Healthcare
- Emphasize compliance (HIPAA), patient data handling, EHR integration.
- Pain: legacy clinical and admin systems blocking modernization.

### Pharma
- Emphasize validation, GxP, regulatory audit trail.
- Pain: slow internal app delivery around R&D, clinical, and commercial operations.

### Financial Services
- Emphasize security, regulatory reporting, core system integration.
- Pain: legacy core systems, fraud and risk tooling, customer experience gaps.

---

## Anti-patterns

Do not write outreach that:
- Leads with Noesis capabilities instead of buyer pain.
- Uses more than one strong personalization variable.
- Pitches before establishing relevance.
- Mixes two personas in one message.
- Includes a calendar link in a cold first-touch email.
